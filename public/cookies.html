Cookie Policy

Last Updated: February 2026

This Cookie Policy explains how Blueprint Compiler ("Service") uses cookies and similar local storage technologies.

1. What Are Cookies and Local Storage?

Cookies are small text files stored on your device when you browse websites. Local storage (`localStorage` and `sessionStorage`) is a more modern web technology that allows web applications to store data directly in your browser without sending it back to the server with every request.

Blueprint Compiler primarily relies on local storage rather than traditional tracking cookies to ensure your data stays secure and private on your device.

2. How We Use These Technologies

We use local storage and session storage for the following essential purposes:

  • Saving Your Progress: We store your wizard selections (e.g., Domain, Target IDE, Stack choices) in `localStorage` so that if you accidentally close the tab, you can resume exactly where you left off.
  • Storing API Keys: When you provide an LLM API key, we store it securely in `sessionStorage`. This ensures the key is only retained while your browser tab is open and is automatically cleared when you close the browser.
  • Authentication: If you use the login feature, Supabase (our authentication provider) uses local storage to keep you securely signed in across sessions.
  • Beta Access: If you unlocked the closed beta, a flag is stored locally to bypass the access wall upon your next visit.

3. Third-Party Cookies

We do not use aggressive third-party advertising cookies. However, some essential third-party services may set cookies:

  • Stripe: If you proceed to a billing page to upgrade your tier, Stripe may set strictly necessary cookies to process payments securely and prevent fraud.
  • Supabase: May use required cookies to manage secure database sessions and user authentication tokens.

4. Managing Your Preferences

Because Blueprint Compiler relies on local storage to function correctly, disabling it via your browser settings will break core features of the app (such as saving progress and maintaining API keys).

If you wish to clear the data we have stored on your device, you can:

  • Use the "Reset" button located in the top navigation bar of the application.
  • Clear your browser's local storage and cache via your browser's privacy or developer settings.

5. Contact

For questions about this policy, please contact us at: privacy@blueprintcompiler.com.
